[Chapter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chapter_(books)) [10](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/10) [Transport](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transport)
[Executive Summary](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Executive_summary)
Meeting [climate mitigation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) [goals](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goal) would require transformative [changes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Change) in the [transport](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transport) [sector](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_sector).
In [2019](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2019), direct [greenhouse gas ( GHG ) emissions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) from the [transport](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transport) [sector](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_sector) were 8.7 [GtCO2 - eq](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change) (up from 5.0 [GtCO2 - eq](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change) in [1990](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1990) ) and accounted for 23 [%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) of global [energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Energy) - related [CO2 emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions).
70 [%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) of direct [transport](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transport) [emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) came from [road vehicles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Motor_vehicle), while 1 [%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage), 11 [%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage), and 12 [%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) came from [rail](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rail_transport), [shipping](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freight_transport), and [aviation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aviation), respectively.
[Emissions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) from [shipping](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freight_transport) and [aviation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aviation) continue to grow rapidly.
[Transport](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transport) - related [emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) in [developing regions of the world](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developing_country) have increased more rapidly than in [Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Europe) or [North America](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_America), a [trend](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fad) that is likely to continue in [coming decades](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Future).
Since the [IPCC 's Fifth Assessment Report ( AR5 )](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IPCC_Fifth_Assessment_Report) there has been a growing [awareness](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Awareness) of the [need](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Need) for [demand management](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demand_management) [solutions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Problem_solving) combined with new [technologies](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technology), such as the rapidly growing use of [electromobility](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electric_vehicle) for [land transport](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_transport) and the [emerging](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emergence) [options](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Choice) in [advanced biofuels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second-generation_biofuels) and [hydrogenbased fuels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydroelectricity) for [shipping](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freight_transport) and [aviation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aviation).
There is a growing [need](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Need) for systemic [infrastructure](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infrastructure) [changes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technological_change) that enable [behavioural](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Behavior) modifications and reductions in [demand](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supply_and_demand) for [transport](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transport) [services](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_service) that can in turn reduce [energy](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Energy) [demand](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supply_and_demand).
The response to the [COVID-19 pandemic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic) has also shown that [behavioural](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Behavior) interventions can reduce [transport](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transport) - related [GHG emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions).
For example , [COVID-19 - based lockdowns](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COVID-19_lockdowns) have [confirmed](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Confirmation) the [transformative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transformative_social_change) [value](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Value) of [telecommuting](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Remote_work) replacing significant [numbers](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Number) of [work](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Employment) and personal [journeys](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Travel) as well as promoting local active [transport](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transport).
There are growing opportunities to implement [strategies](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strategy) that drive [behavioural change](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Behavior_change_(public_health)) and support the [adoption](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adoption) of new [transport](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transport) [technology](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technology) [options](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Choice).
[Changes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_change) in [urban](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urban_culture) form, [behaviour](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Behavior) programmes, the [circular economy](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circular_economy), the [shared economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sharing_economy), and [digitalisation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_transformation) [trends](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fad) can support systemic [changes](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_change) that [lead](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lead) to reductions in [demand](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supply_and_demand) for [transport](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transport) [services](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_service) or expand the use of more efficient [transport modes](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mode_of_transport).
[Cities](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/City) can reduce their [transport](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transport) - related [fuel consumption](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fuel_efficiency) by around 25 [%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) through [combinations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Combination) of more compact [land use](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_use) and the provision of less [car - dependent](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Car_dependency) [transport](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transport) [infrastructure](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infrastructure).
Appropriate [infrastructure](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infrastructure), including [protected](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protection) [pedestrian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pedestrian_zone) and [bike pathways](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bike_lane), can also support much greater localised [active travel](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Active_mobility).
[Transport demand management](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transportation_demand_management) [incentives](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Incentive) are [expected](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Expectation_(epistemic)) to be [necessary](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Need) to support these [systemic](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infrastructure) [changes](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_change).
There is mixed [evidence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evidence) of the effect of [circular economy](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circular_economy) [initiatives](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Initiative), [shared economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sharing_economy) [initiatives](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Initiative), and [digitalisation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_transformation) on [demand](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supply_and_demand) for [transport](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transport) [services](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_service).
For example, while [dematerialisation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dematerialization_(economics)) can reduce the [amount](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantity) of [material](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Material) that [needs](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Need) to be [transported](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transport) to [manufacturing](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manufacturing) [facilities](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Building), an increase in [online shopping](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Online_shopping) with priority [delivery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mail) can increase [demand](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supply_and_demand) for [freight transport](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freight_transport).
Similarly, while [teleworking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Remote_work) could reduce [travel](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tourism) [demand](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supply_and_demand), increased [ridesharing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carpool) could increase [vehicle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vehicle) - [km](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kilometre) [travelled](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Travel).
[Battery electric vehicles ( BEVs )](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battery_electric_vehicle) have lower lifecycle [greenhouse gas emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) than [internal combustion engine](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internal_combustion_engine) [vehicles](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vehicle) (ICEVs) when [BEVs](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battery_electric_vehicle) are [charged](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battery_charger) with [low-carbon electricity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Low-carbon_electricity).
[Electromobility](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electric_vehicle) is being rapidly [implemented](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Implementation) in [micromobility](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Micromobility) ([e - autorickshaws](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electric_rickshaw), [e - scooters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electric_motorcycles_and_scooters), [e - bikes](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electric_bicycle) ), in [transit systems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_transport), especially [buses](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bus), and, to a lesser degree, in the [electrification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electrification) of personal [vehicles](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vehicle).
[BEVs](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battery_electric_vehicle) could also have the added benefit of supporting [grid operations](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electrical_grid).
The [commercial](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trade) availability of [mature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mature_technology) [lithium - ion batteries (LIBs)](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lithium-ion_battery) has [underpinned](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Underpinning) this [growth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_growth) in [electromobility](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electric_vehicle).
As global [battery](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electric_battery) [production](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manufacturing) increases, [unit costs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unit_cost) are declining.
Further [efforts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manual_labour) to reduce the [GHG footprint](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_footprint) of [battery](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electric_battery) [production](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manufacturing), however, are [essential](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Importance) for maximising the [mitigation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) [potential](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Potential) of [BEVs](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battery_electric_vehicle).
The continued [growth](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_growth) of [electromobility](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electric_vehicle) for [land transport](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_transport) would require [investments](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Investment) in [electric charging](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charging_station) and related [grid infrastructure](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electrical_grid).
[Electromobility](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electric_vehicle) [powered](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electric_power) by [low-carbon electricity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Low-carbon_electricity) has the [potential](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Potential) to rapidly reduce [transport GHG](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Environmental_effects_of_transport) and can be [applied](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apply) with multiple [co-benefits](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Co-benefits_of_climate_change_mitigation) in the [developing world](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developing_country) 's [growing](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_growth) [cities](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/City).
[Land](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land) - based, long - range, [heavy-duty](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Truck_classification) [trucks](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Truck) can be decarbonised through [battery electric](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battery_electric_vehicle) [haulage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Haulage) (including the use of [electric road](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electric_road) [systems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_engineering) ), complemented by [hydrogen](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen)- and [biofuel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biofuel) based [fuels](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fuel) in some [contexts](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Context_(language_use)).
These [same](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Identity_(philosophy)) [technologies](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technology) and expanded use of [available](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Availability) [electric rail](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Railway_electrification) [systems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_engineering) can support [rail](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rail_transport) [decarbonisation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation).
Initial [deployments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/System_deployment) of [battery electric](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battery_electric_vehicle) , [hydrogen](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen)- and [bio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biofuel) - based [haulage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Haulage) are underway, and [commercial operations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commercial_vehicle_operation) of some of [these](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/These) [technologies](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technology) are [considered](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Point_of_view_(philosophy)) [feasible](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Logical_possibility) by [2030](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2030s).
[These](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/These) [technologies](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technology) nevertheless face challenges regarding [driving](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Driving) range, [capital](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Capital_(economics)) and [operating costs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operating_cost), and [infrastructure](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infrastructure) [availability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Availability).
In [particular](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Particular), [fuel cell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fuel_cell) [durability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Durability), high [energy consumption](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Energy_consumption), and [costs](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cost) continue to challenge the [commercialisation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commercialization) of [hydrogen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_vehicle) - based [fuel cell vehicles](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fuel_cell_vehicle).
Increased capacity for [low-carbon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Low-carbon_electricity) [hydrogen production](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_production) would also be essential for [hydrogen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen) - based [fuels](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fuel) to [serve](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_service) as an [emissions reduction](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) [strategy](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strategy).
[Decarbonisation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) [options](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Choice) for [shipping](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freight_transport) and [aviation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aviation) still require [R&D](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Research_and_development), though advanced [biofuels](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biofuel), [ammonia](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonia), and [synthetic fuels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Synthetic_fuel) are [emerging](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emergence) as [viable](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Logical_possibility) [options](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Choice).
Increased [efficiency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Efficiency) has been [insufficient](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Necessity_and_sufficiency) to [limit](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) the [emissions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) from [shipping](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freight_transport) and [aviation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aviation), and [natural gas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fuel_gas) - based [fuels](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fuel) are likely inadequate to meet stringent [decarbonisation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) [goals](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goal) for these [segments](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Market_segmentation).
High [energy density](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Energy_density), [low-carbon](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Low-carbon_economy) [fuels](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fuel) are required, but they have not yet reached [commercial scale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mass_production).
[Advanced biofuels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second-generation_biofuels) could provide [low-carbon](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Low-carbon_economy) [jet fuel](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jet_fuel).
The [production](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manufacturing) of [synthetic fuels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Synthetic_fuel) using [low-carbon](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Low-carbon_economy) [hydrogen](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen) with [CO2](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_dioxide) captured through [direct air capture (DAC )](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Direct_air_capture) or [bioenergy with carbon capture and storage ( BECCS )](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bioenergy_with_carbon_capture_and_storage) could provide [jet](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jet_fuel) and [marine](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ocean) [fuels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fuel_oil) but these [options](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Choice) still require [demonstration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technology_demonstration) at scale.
[Ammonia](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonia) [produced](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manufacturing) with [low-carbon](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Low-carbon_economy) [hydrogen](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen) could also [serve](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_service) as a [marine](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ocean) [fuel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fuel_oil).
Deployment of these [fuels](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fuel) [requires](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Need) reductions in [production costs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cost_of_goods_sold).
[Scenarios](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scenario) from [bottom-up and top-down models](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Top-down_and_bottom-up_design) indicate that without [intervention](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation), [CO2](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_dioxide) [emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) from [transport](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transport) could grow in the range of 16 [%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) and 50 [%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) by 2050.
The [scenarios](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_scenario) [literature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Literature) [projects](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Project) continued growth in [demand](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demand) for [freight](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cargo) and [passenger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Passenger) [services](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_service), particularly in [developing countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developing_country) in [Africa](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa) and [Asia](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asia).
This growth is projected to take place across all [transport modes](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mode_of_transport).
Increases in [demand](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demand) notwithstanding, [scenarios](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scenario) that [limit](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) [warming](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change) to 1.5 [°C](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celsius) with no or limited [overshoot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ecological_overshoot) suggest that a  59 [%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) [reduction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) (42 – 68 [%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) [interquartile range](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interquartile_range) ) in [transport](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transport) - related [CO2](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_dioxide) [emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) by 2050, [compared](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comparison) to [modelled](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematical_model) [2020](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020) [levels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantity) is [required](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Requirement).
[While](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/While) many global [scenarios](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scenario) place greater [reliance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_resilience) on [emissions reduction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) in [sectors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_sector) other than [transport](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transport), a quarter of the 1.5 [°C](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celsius) [degree](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Degree_(temperature)) [scenarios](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scenario) [describe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Description) [transport](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transport) - related [CO2](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_dioxide) [emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) [reductions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) in excess of 68 [%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) (relative to [modelled](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematical_model) [2020](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020) [levels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantity) ).
[Illustrative](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Illustration) [mitigation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) [pathways](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_scenario) 1.5 [renewables ( REN )](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Renewable_energy) and 1.5 low [demand](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demand) ( LD ) [describe](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Description) [emission](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) [reductions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) of 80 [%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) and 90 [%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) in the [transport](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transport) [sector](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_sector), respectively, by 2050.
[Transport](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transport) - related [emission](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) [reductions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation), however, may not happen uniformly across [regions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Region).
For example , [transport](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transport) [emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) from the [Developed Countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developed_country) and [Eastern European](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Europe) and [West](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Asia) [Central Asian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Asia) [countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereign_state) decrease from [2020](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020) [levels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantity) by 2050 across all [scenarios](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scenario) [compatible](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Consistency) with a 1.5 [°C](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celsius) [goal](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goal) (C1 – C2 group), but could increase in [Africa](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa), [Asia](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asia) and [Pacific](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean), [Latin America](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin_America) and [Caribbean](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caribbean), and [the Middle East](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_East) in some of these [scenarios](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scenario).

[Chapter](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chapter_(books)) 1 [Introduction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Introduction_(writing)) and [Framing](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Framing_(social_sciences))
[Executive Summary](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Executive_summary)
Global [greenhouse gas](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas) ( [GHG](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) ) [emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) continued to rise to 2019: the aggregate reductions [implied](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Implicature) by current [Nationally Determined Contributions ( NDCs )](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nationally_determined_contribution) to [2030](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2030s) would still make it [impossible](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Impossibility) to [limit warming](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) to 1.5[°C](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celsius) with no or limited overshoot, and would only be compatible with likely [limiting warming](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) below 2[°C](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celsius) [if](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/If_and_only_if) followed by much steeper decline, hence [limiting warming](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) to either level implies [accelerated](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acceleration) [mitigation actions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) at all [scales](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weighing_scale) .
Since the [IPCC 's Fifth Assessment Report](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IPCC_Fifth_Assessment_Report) ([AR5](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IPCC_Fifth_Assessment_Report) ), important changes that have [emerged](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emergence) include the specific [objectives](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goal) established in the [Paris Agreement of 2015](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paris_Agreement) (for [temperature](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temperature), [adaptation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_adaptation) and [finance](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Finance) ), rising [climate impacts](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Effects_of_climate_change), and higher levels of [societal](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Society) [awareness](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Awareness) and support for [climate action](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation).
The growth of global [GHG emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) has slowed over [the past](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Past) [decade](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decade), and delivering the updated [NDCs](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nationally_determined_contribution) to [2030](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2030s) would turn this into decline, but the [implied](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Implicature) global [emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) by [2030](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2030s) exceed pathways [consistent](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Consistency) with 1.5[°C](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celsius) by a large margin, and are near the upper end of the range of modelled pathways which keep [temperatures](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temperature) likely [limit warming](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) to 2[°C](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celsius) (with >65[%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) [probability](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Probability) ).
Continuing [investments](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Investment) in [carbon-intensive activities](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emission_intensity) at scale will heighten the [multitude](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multitude) of [risks](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Risk) [associated](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Correlation) with [climate change](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change) and impede societal and industrial transformation towards [low-carbon](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Low-carbon_economy) development.
Meeting the longterm [temperature](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temperature) [objective](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goal) in the [Paris Agreement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paris_Agreement) therefore [implies](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Logical_consequence) a [rapid](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tachycardia) [turn](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turn_(angle)) to an [accelerating](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acceleration) decline of [GHG](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) [emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) towards ‘[net zero](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_neutrality)', which is implausible without urgent and ambitious action at all [scales](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weighing_scale).
The unprecedented [COVID-19 pandemic](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic) has had far-reaching [impacts](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Effects_of_climate_change) on the global [economic](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_growth) and [social system](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_system), and recovery will present both challenges and opportunities for [climate mitigation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation).
While there are some [trade-offs](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trade-off), effective and equitable [climate policies](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) are largely compatible with the broader [goal](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goal) of [sustainable development](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sustainable_development) and efforts to [eradicate poverty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poverty_reduction) as [enshrined](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shrine) in the 17 [Sustainable Development](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sustainable_development) [Goals](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goal) ([SDGs](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sustainable_Development_Goals) ).
[Climate](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change) [mitigation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) is one of many [goals](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goal) that [societies](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Society) pursue in the [context](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Context_(language_use)) of [sustainable](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sustainable_development) development, as evidenced by the [wide](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wide_area_network) range of the [SDGs](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sustainable_Development_Goals).
[Climate](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change) [mitigation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) has [synergies](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Synergy) and/or [trade-offs](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trade-off) with many other [SDGs](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sustainable_Development_Goals).
[There](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/There_(virtual_world)) has been a [strong](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Base_(chemistry)) [relationship](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Correlation) between development and [GHG](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) [emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions), as [historically](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History) both [per](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persian_language) [capita](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Capita) and absolute [emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) have [risen](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Resurrection) with [industrialisation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Industrialisation).
[However](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/However_(song)), [recent](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Holocene) [evidence](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientific_method) [shows](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Performance) [countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereign_state) [can](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canning) [grow](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_growth) their [economies](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy) while [reducing](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Redox) [emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions).
[Countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Countries_of_the_United_Kingdom) have [different](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Difference_(philosophy)) [priorities](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prioritization) in achieving the [SDGs](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sustainable_Development_Goals) and [reducing](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Redox) [emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) as [informed](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Informed_consent) by their respective national [conditions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disease) and [capabilities](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Capability_approach).
[Given](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shay_Given) the [differences](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sexual_dimorphism) in [GHG](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) [emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) [contributions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donation), [degree](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Academic_degree) of [vulnerabilities](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vulnerability) and [impacts](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Effects_of_climate_change), as well as [capacities](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nameplate_capacity) within and between [nations](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereign_state), [equity](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Justice) and [justice](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_justice) are [important](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Important_Bird_Area) considerations for effective [climate](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) [policy](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Policy) and for securing national and international support for deep [decarbonisation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation).
Achieving [sustainable](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sustainable_development) [global development](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_development) and eradicating [poverty](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poverty) as [enshrined](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shrine) in the 17 SDGS would involve effective and [equitable](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equity_(economics)) [climate](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) [policies](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Policy) at all [levels](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hierarchy) from local to global scale.
[Failure](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Failure) to [address](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_speaking) [questions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prime_Minister%27s_Questions) of [equity](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Justice) and [justice](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_justice) over time [can](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canning) [undermine](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tunnel_warfare) [social cohesion](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Group_cohesiveness) and stability.
[International cooperation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multilateralism) [can](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canning) [enhance](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_enhancement) [efforts](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linguistic_purism) to achieve [ambitious](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ambition_(character_trait)) [global climate](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change) [mitigation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) in the [context](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Context_(language_use)) of [sustainable](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sustainable_development) development.
[The transition](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_transition_to_democracy) to a [low-carbon](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Low-carbon_economy) [economy](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy) [depends](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Speed_of_sound) on a [wide](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wide_area_network) range of closely intertwined [drivers](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Driver%27s_license) and [constraints](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Relational_database), including [policies](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Policy) and [technologies](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technology) where [notable](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Notability) [advances](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Advance_payment) over [the past](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Past) [decade](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decade_(log_scale)) have [opened up](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_economic_reform) new and [large-scale](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Integrated_circuit) [opportunities](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equal_opportunity) for deep [decarbonisation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Low-carbon_economy), and for [alternative](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Renewable_energy) development [pathways](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metabolic_pathway) which could [deliver](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Childbirth) [multiple](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multiple_unit) social and developmental [goals](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goal) ([robust](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robust_statistics) [evidence](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientific_method), [medium](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mass_media) agreement).

political](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_sociology) [issues](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_issue) ([political](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Politics) [economy](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy), [equity](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Justice) and [fairness](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_justice), [social innovation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_innovation) and [behaviour](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Behavior) change), and [institutional](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Institution) factors ([legal](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Law) [framework](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conceptual_framework) and [institutions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Institution), and the [quality](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quality_control) of [international cooperation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_relations) ).
In [addition](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Addition) to being deeply intertwined all the factors [matter](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matter) [to varying degrees](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhenish_fan), depending on the [prevailing](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prevailing_winds) social, [economic](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy), [cultural](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture) and [political](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Politics) [context](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Context_(language_use)).

Over 20 [countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereign_state) have also [sustained](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sustainability) [emission](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Air_pollution) [reductions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reductions), and many more have [accelerated](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acceleration) [energy](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Energy) efficiency and/or [land - use](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_use) [improvements](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_development).

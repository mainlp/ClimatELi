A Relationship between [Climate Finance](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_finance) and [Climate Risk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_risk): [Evidence](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evidence) from the [South Asian Region](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Asia)
Abstract
[South Asia](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Asia) is the most [vulnerable](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_vulnerability) [region](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Region) in the [context](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Context_(language_use)) of [global warming](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change), [climate change](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change), and [climate risk](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_risk).
[Climate finance](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_finance) is the most useful [tool](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tool) for combating [climate](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change) challenges worldwide.
The study explores the present [picture](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Image) of [climate finance](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_finance) in [South Asian (SA)](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Asia) [countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereign_state).
The study uses [multilateral development bank (MDB)](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_financial_institutions), [Green Climate Fund (GCF)](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Green_Climate_Fund), and [Germanwatch](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Germanwatch) supplied [data](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data) from 2011 to 2021.
Under the [theoretical](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theory) [lens](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lens) of [institutional](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Institution) [capacity development](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Capacity_building), the study [attempts](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Attempt) to [correlate](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Correlation) [climate finance](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_finance) and [climate risk](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_risk).
The study indicates an increasing trend of [MDBs](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_financial_institutions) ' and the [GCF](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Green_Climate_Fund) 's [climate finance](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_finance) in many [countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereign_state) worldwide.
The study finds that [MDBs](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_financial_institutions) ' total global [climate finance](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_finance) is [USD](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_dollar) 446,977 [million](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1,000,000), while the [SA region](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Asia) has received [USD](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_dollar) 59,301 [million](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1,000,000) since 2011.
It also reports that [MDBs](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_financial_institutions) provide 77[%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) and 23[%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) of the [money](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Money) to the [mitigation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) and [adaptation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_adaptation) [areas](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Area).
Moreover, the study reports that, after [COVID - 19](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COVID-19), [MDBs](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_financial_institutions) substantially increased the [amount](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantity) of global [climate financing](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_finance), but this increase was [not seen](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unseen_character) in the [SA region](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Asia).
Our [climate risk](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_risk) [data](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data) indicate that most of the [SA](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Asia) [countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereign_state) are highly long-term [climate risky](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_risk) and lose, [on average](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Average), 0.378[%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) of [GDP](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gross_domestic_product).
The [correlation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Correlation) [matrix](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matrix_(mathematics)) finds a negative and [significant](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Statistical_significance) [correlation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Correlation) between [climate finance](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_finance) and long-term and [yearly](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Year) [climate risk](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_risk).
The study identifies that the region's [climate financing](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_finance) flow of [money](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Money) is not [rationally](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rationality) distributed based on the [short-run and long-run](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Long_run_and_short_run) [climate risks](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_risk).
The study presumes that more [climate finance](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_finance) would be the most effective [mechanism](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mechanism_design) to [mitigate](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) [climate risk](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_risk).
Therefore, [SA region](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Asia) [leadership](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leadership) drastically requires a [holistic](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Holism) framework to [address](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_speaking) the prevailing [climate problems](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change) and to ensure regional coordination and [cooperation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cooperative) toward [climate finance](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_finance) and [policies](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Policy).
The [research](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Research) findings have [significant](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Statistical_significance) implications for [climate policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Politics_of_climate_change) and [climate finance](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_finance).
[Keywords](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Index_term): [climate finance](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_finance); [climate risk](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_risk); [South Asia](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Asia); [climate politics](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Politics_of_climate_change)
1. [Introduction](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Introduction_(writing))
[Climate change](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change) and [climate risk](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_risk) are closely [associated](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Correlation) [issues](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_issue) as they directly and indirectly affect [global warming](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change).
[Climate risk](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_risk) is a major challenge in [developing countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developing_country) because of limited [resources](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Resource), lack of capability, ineffective [institutional](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Institution) [atmosphere](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atmosphere), and lack of regional and [international cooperation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_relations) .
Prior studies also reported that [poorer (developing) countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developing_country) are more negatively affected by [climate risks](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_risk) than [richer (developed) countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developed_country) because of several [reasons](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reason), such as (1) [insufficient](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Necessity_and_sufficiency) [resource](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_resource) [capabilities](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Capability_approach) and management, (2) the adversely affected [health](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Health) and [productivity](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Productivity) that decrease national [growth](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_growth), and (3) [financial](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Finance) limitations .
[Global warming](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change), [climate risks](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_risk), and [environmental problems](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pollution) are challenging to [developed economics](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developed_country) and [developing economics](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developing_country) as they [create](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Creativity) [economic inequality](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_inequality) [globally](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Globalization) .
[Climate risk](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_risk) is the [impact](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_impact_on_the_environment) of the [developed countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developed_country)' higher [emission](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) of [CO2](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_dioxide) while [developing countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developing_country) are the ultimate [sufferers](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suffering).
The same argument is [valid](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Validity_(logic)) in the [climate finance](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_finance) mechanism where [developed countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developed_country)' contribution is [insufficient](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Necessity_and_sufficiency), as expected by [developing countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developing_country).
[Climate injustice](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_justice), [climate politics](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Politics_of_climate_change), and [economic inequality](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_inequality) are in the same line of [discrimination](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discrimination) that is highly affected in [developing](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developing_country) and [climate-vulnerable](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_vulnerability) [countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereign_state) .
Globally, [climate justice](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_justice) is a [significant](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Statistical_significance) term because [developing countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developing_country)' [climate risk](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_risk) burden is due to the [developed countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developed_country)' higher [carbon emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions).
Hence, [climate finance](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_finance) is an essential and most effective mechanism to [mitigate](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) global [climate risk](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_risk) and establish [climate justice](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_justice) and [economic equality](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_inequality) .
Another [logical](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Logic) [question](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Question) has been raised as to why do low [greenhouse gas (GHG)](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) [polluting](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pollution) [countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereign_state) face the highest burden of [climate challenges](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change)?
The [International Energy Authority (IEA)](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Energy_Agency) states that [China](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China), [the US](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States), and [India](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India) 's [GHG emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) are responsible for 85[%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) of the world's total [carbon emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions).
It leads to an [academic](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Academic_discipline) [debate](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Debate) on the [injustice](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_justice) of [carbon emission](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) and [economic development](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_development) .
[For](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fornax) [example](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%2eexample), the global [GHG emission](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) of [Bangladesh](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bangladesh) is [less than](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inequality_(mathematics)) 0.35[%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) , while the [country](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereign_state) is the most [vulnerable](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_vulnerability) [country](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereign_state) due to [climate risk](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_risk) driven by [extreme weather](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extreme_weather)  (global [climate risk](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_risk) index is prepared [yearly](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Year) and published by [Germanwatch](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Germanwatch), a Germany-based [international organization](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_organization) ).
It is [estimated](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Estimation) that the [country](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereign_state) will lose 2[%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) and 9.4[%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) of its [gross domestic product (GDP)](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gross_domestic_product) by [2050](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3rd_millennium) and [2100](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3rd_millennium) due to [climate risks](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_risk) .
[South Asia (SA)](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Asia) is the most [vulnerable](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_vulnerability) to [climate change](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change), and most of the [countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereign_state) in the [region](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Region) are highly affected in terms of [environment](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_environment), [water](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Water), [health](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_health), and many other public [issues](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_issue) .
Most of the [countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereign_state) in the [region](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Region) are highly affected by [climate risk](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_risk).
They face severe [problems](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Problem_solving) of [air pollution](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Air_pollution), [water pollution](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Water_pollution) , [public health](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_health), [biodiversity](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biodiversity), [waste](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Waste_management), and [deforestation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deforestation).
[SA region](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Asia) [countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereign_state) are the top [climate-vulnerable](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_vulnerability) [countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereign_state) in the long term and short term (most of the top ten [countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereign_state) are from the [SA region](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Asia), see Table 1) .
Moreover, the region's [CO2](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_dioxide) contribution is much lower than other [developing](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developing_country) and [developed countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developed_country), but the region's [countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereign_state) are paying the most severe [damages](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Damages) due to [global warming](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change) .
Furthermore, major [cities](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/City) are the most [environmentally](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_environment) [polluted](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pollution) in terms of [air](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Air_pollution), [water](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Water_pollution), and [public health](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_health) .
According to [the Economist](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Economist) Intelligence global survey 2021, [Dhaka](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dhaka) is the world's least [livable](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quality_of_life) [capital](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Capital_city) and [the US](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States) [air quality index (AQI)](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Air_quality_index) of 2022 reports that [Dhaka](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dhaka) is the worst city in the world .
According to the [AQI](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Air_quality_index), most of the worst ([polluted](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Air_pollution) ) [cities](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/City) belonging to the [SA region](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Asia) also indicate the [environmental vulnerabilities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Environmental_Vulnerability_Index) of the region's [countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereign_state) (the world's ten most [polluted](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Air_pollution) [cities](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/City) include nine [cities](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/City) in the [SA region](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Asia) ).
Furthermore, [Bangladesh](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bangladesh) faced an [extreme heatwave](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heat_wave) in [April 2023](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023) and [Dhaka](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dhaka) has broken the last fifty-eight years' [temperature records](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_temperature_record) .
[The United Nations Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC)](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intergovernmental_Panel_on_Climate_Change) reported that [climate crises](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_crisis) in [the region](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Region) will be the most challenging [issues](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_issue) for the next two [decades](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decade).
According to the [IPCC](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intergovernmental_Panel_on_Climate_Change) report, [Bangladesh](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bangladesh), [India](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India), [Pakistan](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pakistan), and [Sri Lanka](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sri_Lanka) face [rising sea levels](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_level_rise) and severe [flooding](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flood) while [landlocked](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Landlocked_country) [Afghanistan](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Afghanistan), [Bhutan](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bhutan), and [Nepal](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nepal) face higher [temperatures](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temperature) and [drought](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drought) .
The report also mentions the small [island country](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Island_country) of the [Maldives](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maldives) will be lost in the [future](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Future).
Moreover, the first-ever report of the [United Nations International Children's Emergency Fund (UNICEF)](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UNICEF) on a [children](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Child) 's [climate risk](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_risk) index reported that [children](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Child) in [Afghanistan](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Afghanistan), [Bangladesh](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bangladesh), [India](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India), and [Pakistan](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pakistan) are extremely [vulnerable](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_vulnerability) because of high [temperatures](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temperature) ([heatwaves](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heat_wave) ) and [natural calamities](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_disaster) ([extreme flooding](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flood) ) .
[UNICEF](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UNICEF) also mentioned this [climate crisis](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_crisis) will impact [children](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Child) 's [health](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_health), [education](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Education), and protection.
These negative signals strongly pressure [regulatory bodies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regulatory_agency) and [policymakers](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Policy) to do something for the betterment of these [countries](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereign_state).
Therefore, [policymakers](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Policy) [promulgate](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Promulgation) many [policies](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Policy) and rules to inspire and [motivate](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Motivation) the [financial sector](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Financial_services) to take more action on [climate financing](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_finance).

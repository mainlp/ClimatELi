[Question](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Question) of [Liability](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legal_liability) for Emissions from [Land Development](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_development) in Relation to [New York State](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_(state)) [Climate Change](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change) Plan
Abstract
The [question](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Question) of [liability](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legal_liability) ([responsibility](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_responsibility) ) for [loss and damage (L&D)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Loss_and_damage_(climate_change)) [associated](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Correlation) with [climate change](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change) often ignores the [liability](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legal_liability) for [L&D](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Loss_and_damage_(climate_change)) from [greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) which are the source of [climate change](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change) - related [impacts](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Effects_of_climate_change).
[New York State (NYS)](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_(state)) recognizes its [responsibility](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_responsibility) regarding [climate change](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change) as documented in the [NYS](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_(state)) [Climate Leadership and Community Protection Act (CLCPA)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_Leadership_and_Community_Protection_Act) ([Senate Bill S6599](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_Leadership_and_Community_Protection_Act) ), which put forward the [goal](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goal) of [reducing greenhouse gas emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) from all [anthropogenic sources](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_impact_on_the_environment) 100% over 1990 levels by the year [2050](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3rd_millennium), with an incremental target of at least a 40[%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) [reduction in climate pollution](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) by the year [2030](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2030s).
The current [NYS](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_(state)) [carbon footprint (CF)](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_footprint) does not include soil-based [GHG emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) from [land developments](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_development), preventing the state from reaching its [net-zero emission goals](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_neutrality) .
The current study [addresses](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_speaking) this shortcoming by [quantifying](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantification_(science)) the "realized" [social costs](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_cost) of [CO2 (SC-CO2) emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) for [NYS](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_(state)) from all [land developments](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_development) (12,037.5 [km2](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Square_kilometre), [midpoint](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Midpoint) 1.7 × [1011](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1011) of [total soil carbon (TSC)](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soil_carbon) losses with [midpoint](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Midpoint) [$](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_dollar)28.5B (where B = [billion](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Billion) = 109, [USD](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_dollar) ) ) in [social costs](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_cost) of [carbon dioxide emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions), SC-CO2) and "new" [land developments](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_development) (485.2 [km2](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Square_kilometre) ) in the period from 2001 to 2016, which caused a complete loss of [midpoint](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Midpoint) 6.6 × 109 kg of [TSC](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soil_carbon) resulting in [midpoint](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Midpoint) [$](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_dollar)1.1B SC-CO2.
All [NYS](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_(state)) 's counties experienced [land conversions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_development), with most of the [developments](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_development), [TSC](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soil_carbon) losses, and SC-CO2 occurred near the [existing](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Existence) [urban areas](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urban_area) of [New York City (NYC)](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_City), [Long Island](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Long_Island), and [Albany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albany,_New_York).
[Land conversion](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_development) to [developments](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_development) creates additional [liability](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legal_liability) by the loss of [future](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Future) [GHG](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) [sequestration](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_sequestration) [potential](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Potential_energy) in developed [areas](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Area).
In [addition](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Addition), there is a substantial [future](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Future) [liability](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legal_liability) in [NYS](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_(state)) from [climate change impacts](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Effects_of_climate_change), such as the projected [sea-level rises](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_level_rise) will impact 17 of NY 's 62 counties, which will cause high [costs](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cost) of [adaptation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_adaptation).
[Incorporation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corporation) of [land use](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_use)/[land cover](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_cover) change (LULCC) [analysis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Analysis) can help better [quantify](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantity) the [CF](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_footprint) and identify [ways](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methodology) to reduce [GHG emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) and the [associated](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Correlation) [liabilities](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legal_liability) and compensations to help achieve some of [the United Nations](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations) ([UN](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations) ) [Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sustainable_Development_Goals) .
[Keywords](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Index_term): [carbon](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas); [CO2](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas); compensation; [greenhouse gas](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas); [law](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Law); [policy](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Policy); [sustainable development goals](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sustainable_Development_Goals)
[Introduction](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Introduction_(writing))
[Land development](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_development) results in [land cover](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_cover) change that causes direct [GHG emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions).
[Responsibility](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_responsibility) or [liability](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legal_liability) for the [impacts](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Effects_of_climate_change) of these [GHG emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) can fall to various [parties](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_party), including the [developer](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_development) as well as the local, state, and [federal governments](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federal_government_of_the_United_States), who [permit](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/License) and often even [promote](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marketing) this conversion.
Beyond the [liability](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legal_liability) for the [GHG emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions), [land development](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_development) can incur additional [liability](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legal_liability) [associated](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Correlation) with reduced [future](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Future) [sequestration potential](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_sequestration) (e.g., [loss of the ability](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disability) to further [sequester C](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_sequestration)  in the [soil](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soil) or through [forestry](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forestry) or [agriculture activity](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agriculture) ), as well the [liability](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legal_liability) for [climate impacts](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Effects_of_climate_change) ([Figure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Page_layout) 1).
The [Role](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Role) of [Soils](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soil) [in New York State](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_(state)) 's [Plan](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plan) to [Reduce Greenhouse  Gas (GHG) Emissions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation)
On June 18, 2019, the [NYS](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_(state)) issued [Senate Bill S6599](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_Leadership_and_Community_Protection_Act), "[New York State](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_(state)) [Climate Leadership and Community Protection Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_Leadership_and_Community_Protection_Act) " ([CLCPA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_Leadership_and_Community_Protection_Act) ), which sets [GHG emission reduction](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) targets from all [anthropogenic sources](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_impact_on_the_environment) 100% over 1990 levels by the year [2050](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3rd_millennium), with an incremental target of at least a 40[%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) [reduction in climate pollution](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation) by the year [2030](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2030) (Table 1) .
The principal sources of [NYS](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_(state)) 's 2019 gross [GHG emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) were: [transportation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transport) (29[%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) ), [electricity](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electricity) (13[%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) ), [industry](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Industry_(economics)) (9[%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) ), [buildings](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Construction) (32[%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) ), [agriculture](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agriculture) (6[%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) ), and [waste](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Waste) (11[%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) )  .
This list does not include soil-based [GHG emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) from [land conversions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_development).
[New York State](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_(state)) 's [pedodiversity (soil diversity)](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pedodiversity) is represented by seven [soil](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soil) orders, belonging to slightly [weathered](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weathering) [soils](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soil) ([Entisols](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entisol), [Inceptisols](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inceptisol), [Histosols](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Histosol) ), moderately [weathered](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weathering) [soils](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soil) ([Alfisols](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alfisol), [Mollisols](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mollisol) ), and strongly [weathered](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weathering) [soils](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soil) ([Spodosols](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Podzol), [Ultisols](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ultisol) ) with different [soil](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soil) [ecosystem services and disservices (ES/ED)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ecosystem_service) and [climate change vulnerabilities](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_vulnerability) (Table 2 and [Figure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Page_layout) 2).
The [NYS](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_(state)) has picked [Honeoye](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Honeoye,_New_York) as [the State](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_(polity)) [Soil](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soil) ([soil](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soil) order: [Alfisols](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alfisol) ) because of its [agricultural](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agriculture) [importance](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Importance) (e.g., [corn](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maize), [wheat](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wheat), [soybeans](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soybean), [oats](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oat), [alfalfa](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alfalfa), [vegetables](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vegetable), [grapes](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grape), [apples](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple), [grass](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poaceae) [pasture](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pasture), [hay](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hay), [etc](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Et_cetera) ).
[Soils](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soil) of [NYS](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_(state)) provide various [ecosystem services](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ecosystem_service) ([provisional](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Provisional_government), [regulation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regulation) / [maintenance](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Road), and [cultural](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture) ) within the [economic](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy) [regions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Region) of the state (Table 2).
Most of the [economic](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy) [regions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Region) are dominated by the [soil](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soil) order of [Inceptisols](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inceptisol) (54[%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) for the state), except for the [Finger Lakes region](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Finger_Lakes), which is [dominated](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dominant-party_system) by [Alfisols](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alfisol) (55.6[%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) ) (Table 3).
As one of the leading producers of [GHG emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) (more than all [Central America](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_America) and [Mexico](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexico) combined), [NYS](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_(state)) has been experiencing numerous [climate change impacts](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Effects_of_climate_change): increasing [ocean](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ocean_temperature) and [atmospheric temperatures](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atmospheric_temperature); increase in the occurrence of [extreme weather events](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extreme_weather) and their intensity; [rising sea levels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_level_rise) and [loss of land](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_loss) (especially [existing](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Existence) [wetlands](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wetland) ) because of retreating [shores](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coast) and [land surface sinking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change); [coastal storms](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Storm) and [flooding](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flood) .
These [climate-change-related L&D](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Loss_and_damage_(climate_change)) result in [significant](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Statistical_significance) non-economic (incalculable) and [economic losses](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/pure_economic_loss) in [NYS](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_(state)) .
For example, historical [property damage](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Property_damage) [cost](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cost) [data](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data) (1960–2014, total damage: [$](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_dollar)25.56B in 2014 [USD](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_dollar) ) shows the following [damage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Damage) causes: [flooding](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flood) ([$](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_dollar)7.11B, 27.8[%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) ), [severe storms](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Storm) ([$](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_dollar)4.30B, 16.8[%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) ), [winter](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Winter_storm) [storms](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Storm) ([$](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_dollar)3.15B, 12.3[%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) ), [hurricanes Floyd](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hurricane_Floyd) and [Irene](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hurricane_Irene) ([$](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_dollar)240.43M, 0.9[%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) ), [Hurricane Sandy](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hurricane_Sandy) ([$](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_dollar)10.75B, 42.1[%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) ) .
Overall, [hurricanes](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropical_cyclone) accounted for 43[%](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percentage) of the total [damages](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Damages) [impacting](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Impact_event) primarily [NYC](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_City) and [Long Island](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Long_Island) ([$](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_dollar)11.32B in 2014 [USD](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_dollar) ) .
The present study [hypothesizes](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hypothesis) that [liability](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legal_liability) is a [responsibility](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duty) for [climate change](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change) from [GHG emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions), which can be elucidated, quantified, and valued based on the [social cost](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_cost) of [emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) (e.g., the [social cost](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_cost) of [CO2 emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions), SC-CO2, [etc](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Et_cetera) ).
[Land conversion](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_development) causes [GHG emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) from [land development](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_development) as well as any [future](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Future) loss of [soil](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soil) [C sequestration potential](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_sequestration) because of the [consequences](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Effects_of_climate_change) of [land development](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_development) (e.g., [buildings](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Construction), [impervious areas](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Impervious_surface), [etc](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Et_cetera) ).
[Liability](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legal_liability) for these [emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) and the subsequent [climate change L&D](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Loss_and_damage_(climate_change)) can be divided among various [responsible](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legal_liability) [parties](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_party), including the [administrative entities](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Administrative_division) that create [regulations](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regulation) and [permit](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/License) [development](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_development), the [developers](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_development) that cause the [land conversion](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_development) and [GHG emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions), and the [consumers](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Consumer) who purchase the developed [property](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Property).
Partial [liability](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legal_liability) from [GHG emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) can be quantified and valued as SC-CO2 at a fixed rate of [$](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_dollar)46 per [metric ton](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tonne) of [CO2](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas) , which can be potentially used to help assign [liability](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legal_liability) for [climate change impacts](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Effects_of_climate_change).
The fixed [cost](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cost) of SC-CO2, in many cases, will not [compensate](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Financial_compensation) for the actual [market-based](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Market_economy) [costs](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cost) of [L&D](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Loss_and_damage_(climate_change)) for which [liability](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legal_liability) will need to be assigned.
Additionally, there are non-economic [liabilities](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legal_liability) (e.g., [trauma](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Injury), loss of culture, [etc](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Et_cetera) ), as well as reduced [future](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Future) [sequestration potential](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_sequestration) of developed [lands](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land).
Assigning this [liability](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legal_liability) has added [complexity](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Complexity) because, while attributing [GHG emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) is [possible](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Logical_possibility) (and demonstrated in this [research](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Research) ), linking [specific](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sensitivity_and_specificity) [climate L&D](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Loss_and_damage_(climate_change)) events to individual [GHG emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions) is an [emerging](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emergence) [science](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Science); however, [feedback mechanisms](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Feedback) are [necessary](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Need) to change [behavior](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Behavior) that results in [GHG emissions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions).
Assignment of [liability](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legal_liability) may be [necessary](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Need) to create new [legal frameworks](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legal_doctrine) to change [behavior](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Behavior) that leads to [climate change](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change) as well as create [funding mechanisms](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Funding) for [L&D](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Loss_and_damage_(climate_change)) compensation.
There are various [techniques](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technology) to attribute and [estimate](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Approximation) [liability](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legal_liability) across different [geographic](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geography) extents, time periods, and [entities](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legal_person), which we will discuss in this [paper](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paper).
